## What an Affiliate relationship means

StackBriefs may have a commercial relationship with the destination or network behind an Affiliate Offer. If you use an Offer Link, StackBriefs may receive compensation under that relationship. The relationship applies to the optional outbound link; it does not turn the Tool, its price, or its product claims into an endorsement.

StackBriefs does not require a commercial click for the decision to remain useful. You can review the Scenario context, matching explanations, limitations, evidence, hands-on status, and neutral Official Link whether or not a qualifying Offer is available.

## Three link types

Official, Evidence, and Offer links answer different questions and remain structurally separate.

A free plan, paid plan, trial, or price is a product fact. It is not automatically an Affiliate Offer and does not create an Offer Link by itself.

## When an Offer appears

StackBriefs renders an Affiliate Offer only when the public Offer projection qualifies at the current check date. The supported public statuses are **Verified deal** and **Trackable affiliate offer**. Current evidence must support the status, match the Offer and material scope, and remain within the seven-day freshness window used for deals and tracking.

The Offer panel keeps the commercial path separate from product evidence. It shows the public status, recorded terms when available, relevant region when present, check date, supporting Evidence Link, Offer Link, and compensation disclosure. A **Verified deal** label means current qualifying evidence supports the Offer status; it is not a conclusion about whether the Tool fits your Scenario.

Offer status can change after the static page is built. Browser checks may remove an Offer whose evidence or validity window has expired, but they cannot make an unqualified Offer appear.

## When no Offer appears

Research-only, expired, rejected, stale, future-dated, wrong-scope, missing, invalid, or unverifiable commercial records do not render an active Offer Link. They also cannot produce current Deal or Discount language.

When no qualifying Offer appears, the neutral Official Link remains available as the default vendor destination. StackBriefs does not hide the Official Link, replace it with a commercial destination, or treat the absence of an Offer as a negative product fact.

## Compensation and sponsored labeling

Every active Offer panel states that **StackBriefs may receive compensation if you use this link**. The statement appears next to the Offer Link so you can understand the relationship before leaving StackBriefs.

The Offer Link carries a `sponsored` relationship label for browsers and search systems. It also uses opener protection when it opens a new tab. Official Links and Evidence Links remain separately labeled and do not inherit the sponsored relationship simply because an Offer exists on the same Tool page.

Compensation terms, Affiliate network identity, and commercial value are not decision inputs. StackBriefs does not publish commission reporting in the current product.

## Commercial neutrality

Commercial data cannot create a candidate, resolve unknown evidence, change eligibility or order, alter matching explanations, select alternatives, or change comparison conclusions. Offer presence, Affiliate status, network, commission, and commercial value have the same prohibition.

Payments, vendor submissions, and commercial relationships are intake or presentation metadata only. They do not bypass evidence authority, scope, freshness, publication, or correction checks. Removing or changing only Affiliate data must leave the Scenario decision output unchanged.

For the matching and evidence rules that remain outside this disclosure, read the separate [Methodology](/methodology).
